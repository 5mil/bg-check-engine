Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. What We Collect

  • GitHub OAuth token — stored in your browser's localStorage only, never on our servers
  • Search inputs — first name, last name, state, date of birth — transmitted to public record APIs to fulfill your query
  • No analytics, no tracking pixels, no cookies are set by this application

2. How Data Is Used

Search inputs are forwarded to third-party public record sources (CourtListener, NSOPW, OpenSanctions, JudyRecords) solely to return results to you. We do not store, log, or aggregate search queries.

3. Third-Party Services

  • GitHub OAuth — login is handled via GitHub's OAuth flow. GitHub's Privacy Statement applies.
  • CourtListener — Free Law Project. See their privacy policy.
  • NSOPW — U.S. Department of Justice federal system.
  • OpenSanctions — See opensanctions.org/docs/privacy.
  • Stripe Identity / Onfido — Only invoked when you explicitly launch a verification session. Their respective privacy policies apply.

4. Data Retention

No search data is retained server-side. Your GitHub token is stored in localStorage and cleared on sign-out. You can clear it at any time by signing out or clearing browser storage.

5. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights under GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, or other applicable laws to access, correct, or delete personal data. Since we do not store personal data server-side, the primary data controller for any retained data is your browser and the third-party services listed above.

6. Contact

Questions? Open an issue at github.com/5mil/bg-check-engine/issues.
